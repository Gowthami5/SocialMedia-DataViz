  • 1960s Email

    The first example of email can be found on computers at MIT in a program called “MAILBOX”, all the way back in 1965. Users of MIT computers could leave messages with this program on computers at the university for other users, who would see the messages the next time they logged on to the computer.

  • 1970s File sharing

    Files were first exchanged on removable media. Computers were able to access remote files using filesystem mounting, bulletin board systems (1978), Usenet (1979), and FTP servers (1970's). Internet Relay Chat (1988) and Hotline (1997) enabled users to communicate remotely through chat and to exchange files.

  • 1982 TCP/IP

    TCP/IP was standardized. TCP/IP was developed in the 1970s and adopted as the protocol standard for ARPANET (the predecessor to the Internet) in 1983. It is the set of communications protocols used for the Internet and other similar networks.

  • 1989 World Wide Web

    By December 1990, Berners-Lee and his work team had built all the tools necessary for a working Web: the HyperText Transfer Protocol (HTTP), the HyperText Markup Language (HTML), the first web browser (named WorldWideWeb, which was also a web editor), the first web server (later known as CERN httpd) and the first web site (http://info.cern.ch) containing the first web pages that described the project itself was published on 20 December 1990.

  • 1990 Website

    first web browser and website launched. The first website at CERN – and in the world – was dedicated to the World Wide Web project itself and was hosted on Berners-Lee's NeXT computer. In 2013, CERN launched a project to restore this first ever website: info.cern.ch. On 30 April 1993, CERN put the World Wide Web software in the public domain.

  • 2000s Social Media

    Begins with the short-lived Six Degrees profile uploading service in 1997, and followed in 2001 by Friendster. These rudimentary platforms attracted millions of users and enabled email address registration and basic online networking. Weblogs, or blogs, another early form of digital social communication, began to gain popularity with the 1999 launch of the LiveJournal publishing site. This coincided with the launch of the Blogger publishing platform by the tech company Pyra Labs, which was purchased by Google in 2003.

  • 2007 Facebook marketplace

    Facebook shops are the new way to shop online. With more than 2.89 billion monthly active users, brands are taking advantage of the traffic it brings. The Facebook marketplace started in 2007, expanding on the first 'Buy Button' launched in 2014. But, they didn't stop there. Instead, they saw the potential of e-commerce and opened the platform for user-direct selling in 2018.

  • 2015 Instagram "Shop Now"

    Instagram is also a platform that is constantly changing and evolving. Acquired by Facebook last 2012 for $1 billion, it now contributes a staggering $20 billion in revenue to its parent company. It started with a simple CTA "Shop Now" button in 2015. It continued development with the complete Instagram shopping platform in 2018.

  • TikTok

    Tiktok is a platform that has become popular among the younger generation. It's a video-sharing application that allows users to create and share short videos, often called "Tiktok" videos, which can be about anything from makeup tutorials to cooking. In 2021, TikTok generated $4 billion in advertising revenue. Analysts project the platform's income will increase to about $13.8 billion by 2026.
